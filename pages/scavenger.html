The Scavenger's Scrapbook

The early days of the collapse, where survivors use the last salvaged remnants of the old world. The scene is a dimly lit bunker, illuminated by a warm, flickering oil lamp. On a dented metal tray, we see the rough textures of the Canned-Clad Ratatouille, a chaotic stew of wilted greens and pale, canned vegetable chunks. Beside it is a cracked ceramic bowl holding the Twinkie-Crust Cobbler, a desperate dessert where the iconic golden, spongy snack cakes melt over dark, preserved berries. This lighting and grungy aesthetic establish the visual base for our journey.

Canned-Clad Ratatouille

  • Wilted Greens & Garden Scraps: Hand-picked, slightly bruised or overgrown wild greens.
  • Pale Canned Vegetable Chunks: Salvaged potatoes or root vegetables from expired cans.
  • Base: 1 can of condensed tomato soup (used as the stewing broth).

Twinkie-Crust Cobbler

  • Golden Snack Cakes (Twinkies): 3 or 4 cakes, stale but structurally sound, torn into rough chunks to serve as the "biscuits".
  • Dark Preserved Berries: A handful of scavenged wild blackberries or a jar of pre-collapse pie filling.
